Research Interests

  • Planning Theory and Practice
  • Governance and Regional Planning
  • Development and planning regulation

Phil Allmendinger has published extensively in the areas of property and planning, planning theory, policy and practice, land and property regulation, housing and local government. His research thus spans across several of the Department‘s interests, especially in planning, property and housing. Professor Allmendinger is a chartered planner and surveyor. He initially trained as a spatial planner and has also worked in local government. He is a member of the Economic and Social Research Council Research Grants Board and a member of the Communities and Local Government Housing Markets

Recent Research projects

Health and Safety Executive (HSE) Economic Costs of Land Use Restrictions around Major Hazard Sites (with NLP) (2010)

RICS: Transaction Costs, Planning and Housing Supply. June 2008 – June 2009.

CLG: Market Information and Planning for Economic Development. June – July 2008.

ESRC: Housing Supply and Planning Delay (with Professor Michael Ball, University of Reading). 2006-2008.

ESRC: Integrated Spatial Planning, Multi-level Governance and State Rescaling (with Professor Graham Haughton of the University of Hull, Dr. Dave Counsell of the University of Cork and Dr. Geoff Vigar, University of Newcastle). January 2005-December 2007

ODPM: Future Direction of Mobile Phone and Electronic Communications Network Technology (with Arup). 2006

RICS Foundation: The ‘Fourth Way’: Partnership Based Alternatives to Development Control in UK Planning. 2007

ODPM: Rethinking the Planning Regulation of Land and Property Markets (with Professor Michael Ball) (2005-2006).
